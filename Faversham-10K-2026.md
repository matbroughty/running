## Plan philosophy

This plan adapts Runner's World's intermediate 10K methodology to a 17-week build (their template is six weeks and assumes a faster current base) shaped to your fixed Mon–Sun schedule. The principles guiding it:

1. **Easy is truly easy.** 1–2 minutes per km slower than 5K pace. Conversational. If your easy days aren't easy, your quality days can't be quality. This is the single most common mistake.
2. **Variety in the key session.** Ladders, descending sets, mixed-pace, pyramids, threshold blocks. Each shape teaches the body something different and stops the plan feeling stale.
3. **Race-pace volume from week 9.** Once base and threshold are built, the body has to learn 4:30/km. Familiarity beats heroics.
4. **Strides keep speed in the legs.** Cheap, short, no fatigue cost. 4–6 × 100 m after most easy runs.
5. **Tempo builds the engine.** 15–30 minutes at 4:45–5:00/km is the work that lets you hold race pace for 10 km.
6. **Long runs build durability.** Time on feet at easy pace. The 16 km long run prepares for race-day aerobic load.
7. **Weight follows training.** Easy days truly easy = more fat-burning, less compensatory hunger. Don't underfuel quality sessions; that's where adaptation happens. Aim for ~1 kg per fortnight, not faster.

## Workout library

Reusable session definitions. Each week's plan references these by name.

### Easy run
- **Pace:** 5:50 – 6:15 / km
- **Purpose:** Aerobic base, capillary development, recovery from quality sessions
- **Feel:** Conversational — full sentences without breaking
- **Adaptation:** Mitochondrial density, fat oxidation, capillary growth
- **Notes:** If unsure, go slower. Most runners run easy days too hard.

### Long run
- **Pace:** 5:45 – 6:15 / km (closer to 6:00 on longer ones)
- **Purpose:** Aerobic endurance, durability, mental rehearsal of being out on feet
- **Feel:** Easy for the first half. Last 3–4 km should still feel controlled but you know you've worked.
- **Adaptation:** Glycogen storage, fat utilisation, slow-twitch fibre recruitment
- **Notes:** Distance over pace. If the legs are heavy, drop to 6:15/km — don't skip.

### Strides
- **Format:** 4–6 × 100 m
- **Effort:** Accelerate gradually over 100 m to ~90 % effort, hold 5 seconds, smoothly decelerate. Walk to full recovery between reps.
- **Purpose:** Maintain neuromuscular sharpness, improve running economy, light "pop" without fatigue
- **Adaptation:** Form refinement, fast-twitch recruitment
- **Notes:** Not all-out. You're rehearsing speed, not testing it.

### 400 m repeats
- **Pace:** 5K pace (~1:46–1:48 per rep)
- **Recovery:** 90 sec jog or 200 m
- **Purpose:** VO₂ max introduction, leg turnover
- **Feel:** Hard middle, controlled. Each rep should feel like you could do "one more."
- **Used in:** Base block (early), recovery week

### 800 m repeats
- **Pace:** 5K to 10K pace (~3:30 – 3:40 per rep)
- **Recovery:** 2 min jog or 400 m
- **Purpose:** VO₂ max, sustainable speed
- **Feel:** Hard but holdable. Don't fade by the last rep — pace the first one a touch slower than you think.
- **Used in:** Base block, recovery week

### 1 km repeats — threshold
- **Pace:** 4:45 – 5:00 / km (tempo)
- **Recovery:** 90 sec – 2 min jog
- **Purpose:** Lactate threshold development
- **Feel:** "Comfortably uncomfortable." Sustainable. Last rep should feel like the first.
- **Used in:** Threshold block

### 1 km repeats — race pace
- **Pace:** 4:30 / km exactly
- **Recovery:** 90 sec jog
- **Purpose:** Race-pace endurance, mental rehearsal
- **Feel:** Familiar after a few weeks of it. First km should feel almost easy; by rep 4 it bites.
- **Used in:** Race-specific block

### Hill repeats
- **Format:** 8 × 60 seconds uphill, hard effort. Jog or walk down for recovery.
- **Pace:** Effort-based, not pace-based
- **Purpose:** Leg strength, VO₂ max, forced good form
- **Feel:** Lungs burn, legs work. Take full recovery — no rush.
- **Used in:** Base block (week 3)

### Ladder
- **Format:** Reps lengthen then shorten, e.g. 400 / 800 / 1200 / 800 / 400
- **Pace:** Constant target pace across all reps (race pace or threshold, depending on block)
- **Recovery:** Half the interval distance, jog
- **Purpose:** Pace discipline at varied durations
- **Feel:** The longest rep in the middle is the test. If you've paced the climbs sensibly, the descent is doable.
- **Used in:** Base block (intro), race-specific block (signature session at race pace)

### Descending intervals
- **Format:** Reps get shorter as the session progresses, e.g. 1200 / 1000 / 800 / 600 / 400
- **Pace:** Constant tempo / race pace, or progressive (each rep slightly faster)
- **Recovery:** 90 sec – 2 min jog
- **Purpose:** Mental — the workout "shrinks" as you tire. Finishes feel doable.
- **Feel:** Each rep gets shorter but intensity holds or rises. Strong finish.
- **Used in:** Threshold and race-specific blocks

### Pyramid
- **Format:** Up then back down, e.g. 200 / 400 / 600 / 400 / 200
- **Pace:** Constant or progressive
- **Recovery:** Half-distance or equal-distance jog
- **Purpose:** Variety; gentler intro to ladders
- **Feel:** The back half mirrors the front and feels "downhill."
- **Used in:** Base block (intro), sharpening (peak version)

### Mixed interval session
- **Format:** Mixes race-pace blocks with shorter speed work, e.g. 1×1200 RP + 2×800 RP + 4×200 RP + 4×200 speed + 4 strides
- **Pace:** Race pace (4:30/km) for the bulk, speed pace (4:20/km) for the short reps
- **Recovery:** Half-distance jog between reps
- **Purpose:** Combines race-pace endurance with sharpening
- **Feel:** Long reps challenge endurance. Short fast reps feel relatively easy because they're short.
- **Used in:** Race-specific block (Runner's World signature session)

### Speed broken
- **Format:** Bookended longer reps with dense shorter middle, e.g. 1×800 + 4×400 + 4×200 + 1×800
- **Pace:** Speed pace (4:20 / km)
- **Recovery:** Half-distance jog
- **Purpose:** VO₂ max, leg-speed under fatigue
- **Feel:** The closing 800 is the test — drive hard when tired.
- **Used in:** Sharpening (week 15, late in plan)

### Tempo / threshold run
- **Format:** Continuous 15 – 30 minutes at tempo pace
- **Pace:** 4:45 – 5:00 / km
- **Purpose:** Lactate threshold development
- **Feel:** "Comfortably uncomfortable." You can talk in fragments, not sentences.
- **Used in:** Threshold and race-specific blocks (Fridays)

### Split tempo
- **Format:** 2 × 10 min or 2 × 12 min at tempo pace, with 3 min easy jog between
- **Pace:** 4:45 – 5:00 / km
- **Purpose:** Threshold work with a sharper average pace because of the break
- **Feel:** The second block can feel harder than the first — fight to hold the same pace.
- **Used in:** Threshold and race-specific blocks (Fridays)

### Race-pace rehearsal (Parkrun)
- **Format:** 5 km at exactly 4:30 / km
- **Purpose:** Test race pace on tired legs and learn what it feels like
- **Feel:** First km feels almost easy. By km 4 the discipline is mental.
- **Used in:** Sharpening (week 14)
